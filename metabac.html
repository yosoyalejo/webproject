The Metatranscriptome of the Rainbow-Trout Microbiota

RainbowTrout

  • Scientific name: Oncorhynchus mykiss
  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Class: Actinopterygii
  • Order: Salmoniformes
  • Family: Salmonidae
  • Genus: Oncorhynchus

The bacterial community in the intestines of fish, the so-called gut microbiome, interacts in various ways with the metabolism and immune system of its host. However, there is no precise description of these interactions, especially for commercially used fish species. Microbiome research in aquaculture has so far mainly focused on the structural composition of the bacterial community (who is there?) And hardly on its function (what can it do?). Previous studies have already shown that the husbandry environment, genetic origin, physiological changes, stress levels and, above all, feed have an effect on the structural composition of the microbiome. The planned research project therefore focuses on a functional characterization of the fish microbiome through a metatranscriptome analysis of the intestinal microbiome and examines both the structure and the functional properties of the intestinal microbiome of rainbow trout depending on various feeding strategies and continuous stress. Above all, own preliminary work on the influence of feed on the intestinal microbiome and previous research results on the effect of the bacterial community on the stress resistance of fish form the basis for the hypothesis that the microbiome could establish a connection between nutrient supply and stress resistance. Successful aquaculture is based above all on an optimal supply of nutrients and a stress-free housing environment. In commercial fish farms in particular, chronic husbandry stress has a strong effect on fish growth and thus on the success of production, despite optimal feeding. Since earlier studies also suggest an influence of the genetic background on the composition of the microbiome, this is also considered by directly comparing trout from two different breeding lines in a special test approach. The data set to be determined will not only enable a structural analysis of the microbial community in the fish intestine (who is there?), But above all also a functional characterization of the microbiome (what can it do?), And will thus create an important gap in understanding Infer the structure and function of the microbiome in aquaculture species.
